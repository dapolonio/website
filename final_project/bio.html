Biography

Home Recordings Gallery Lifestyle

Daniel Apolonio, 20, is a violinist from Southern California and lives in the East Village in Manhattan, NY. He is concertmaster of his university’s orchestra. Previous awards include first place in the Southern California Concerto Competition and the preliminary round of the 2019 ASTA-LA Solo Competition, finalist in the New York University concerto competition, and Third place in the 2019 Pacific Symphony Youth Orchestra Concerto Competition. He has performed in venues such as Thayer Hall at Colburn, Harris Concert Hall in Aspen, and Gilles-Lefebvre Concert Hall in Québec. As an orchestra musician, he has been a member of many ensembles, allowing him to perform in venues such as the Renée and Henry Segerstrom Concert Hall, Musco Center for the Arts, and Glinka Capella in Saint Petersburg. He has played in masterclasses for artists such as Stefan Jackiw, Nathan Cole, David Kim, and Haik Kazazyan. He has also performed in chamber music masterclasses for Peter Marsh as well as the Kronos and Pacifica Quartets. Daniel attended the Orange County School of the Arts for the entirety of middle and highschool, in addition to having attended the Aspen Music Festival in the summer. He began studying the violin at the age of 10 and currently studies with Giora Schmidt and Gregory Fulkerson at NYU. Previous teachers include Sam Fischer, Robert Chen, and Connie Heard.
